In May 1980, Lynn Walker was in college, aspiring to a future in music and spirituality. He purchased a copy of Srila Prabhupada's Bhagavad Gita As-it-is from a used bookstore. Two months later Lynn met Laurel Wright, also in college. They married one year later and within weeks began visiting an ISKCON temple in Berkeley, California. They soon gave up their jobs, car, apartment and moved to an ISKCON farm community (Mt. Kailasa) in Northern California. They were initiated and remarried by Hansadutta Swami in May 1982, receiving the spiritual names Laghu Bhagavatamrta dasa and Satarupa devi dasi in a Vedic fire sacrifice.

Hansadutta instructed the two disciples to “Have one hundred children and raise them to be Krishna Conscious.”

Also at the fire sacrifice were members of the ISKCON Berkeley Traveling Sankirtana Party. This was the first time Laghu and Satarupa met Titiksava Karunika (TK). TK performed a collection of songs for the guru, Hansadutta Swami, accompanied by other members of the Sankirtana Party. At least one of those songs, "Animals Animals", has yet to be formally released by the artist.

Laghu and Satarupa lived within the Berkeley temple community for the next few years. Kristen Sarasvati was born in 1984 and given the name Krishna Sankirtana by Hansadutta. A second daughter, Tulsi, was born in 1986. Laghu and Satarupa met another pair of Berkeley Hansadutta disciples with musical interests during this period, Lokanatha and his wife Maharani.

Laghu Bhagavatamrta and Satarupa received second initiation from Rohini Kumar Swami in 1985. He instructed them to: “Chant Hare Krishna and be happy.”

In August 1996 Laghu, Satarupa and their daughters traveled to Los Angeles to attend the Srila Prabhupada Centennial Appearance Day Festival. Inspired by bhajans and kirtanas they attended, they were encouraged to begin singing and playing bhajans. "Lotus" was Tulsi's idea for the name of their musical group.

In June 1997 Lotu's held it's first formal live performance, taking the stage at ISKCON of Santa Cruz's Panihati Festival.

In 1997 Laghu approached their Guru Maharaja, by then named Bhaktivaidurya Madhava Swami, with an offer. If he would come to Santa Cruz County to start a temple they would sponsor the endeavor.

Their Guru Maharaja responded: “Santa Cruz County is too hippie-dippie, but I would start a temple in San Jose.” Thus ISKCON of Silicon Valley was established.

Laghu and his family became regular attendees as well as sponsers of ISKCON of Silicon Valley, participating in weekly programs, festivals, even aspects of the Abhay Charan Video Series as it's production of the San Francisco memories were filmed. In August 1998, Lotu's opened the program for ISKCON of Silicon Valley's Janmastami Festival. The album of this performance at the Campbell Community Center in Campbell, California represent the only remaining recordings of the original Lotu's musical group. A young Australian-born devotee, Sridhama Mayapur, joined the family on mridanga for this performance.

Also in 1998, Sridhama Mayapur and Laghu collaborated on an additional selection of Vaishnava songs.

In 1999, Lokanatha and Laghu collaborated on 20 Vaishnava songs, primarily versions of the Maha Mantra melody

Lokanatha Prabhu has demonstrated exceptional skill with musical transcribing, assembling a collection of at least 200 versions of the Maha Mantra in standard musical notation. Any person interested in receiving a copy of these melodies may contact Lotu's for information.

In 2000, Laghu and Titiksava Karunika (TK) collaborated on a set of four songs in the popular music format. Already a popular recording artist, TK included at least one of these songs on a later album.

Lotu's
Musicians

In addition to the original family
of four musicians, Lotu's has enjoyed
the participation of other outstanding
ISKCON devotees.

The original Lotu's performers were from one family. With the second live performance, the Janmastami Festival at ISKCON of Silicon Valley, Lotu's added a talented young mridanga player, Sridhama Mayapur, who later joined the group for additional recorded material.

The next year Lotu's collobarated with talented ISKCON bhajan artist, Lokanatha das Prabhu for a collection of twenty Vaishnava melodies, mostly the Maha Mantra. In the year following, Titiksava Karunika collobarated with Laghu on the writing and recording of four songs in the popular music genre.

The family has since grown by two members. The eldest, Bhakta Alexander, has become adept at harmonium and mridanga. The day is eagerly anticipated when Bhakta Alexander and his younger sister, Bhaktin Claire, will join their parents and older sisters in singing and playing Vaishnava bhajan music.

Lotu's is always open to the participation of other talented and enthusiastic Vaishnava musicians and singers.
